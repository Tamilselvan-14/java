Phishing attacks have emerged as one of the most critical cybersecurity threats in cloud-based environments, where massive amounts of sensitive data and resources are stored and accessed remotely. The increasing adoption of cloud services by businesses and individuals has created a larger attack surface for cybercriminals, making traditional static security measures insufficient. This research proposes an adaptive framework for the prediction and detection of phishing attacks that leverages machine learning and intelligent data analytics to address these challenges. The framework incorporates multiple layers of protection, including data pre-processing, feature extraction, real-time monitoring, and predictive modelling, to identify suspicious activities and malicious patterns with high accuracy. By employing adaptive learning techniques, the system continuously updates its detection models to recognize emerging phishing tactics, ensuring resilience against zero-day attacks. Cloud- based deployment enables scalability, faster threat response, and seamless integration with existing infrastructure, thereby reducing downtime and security risks. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed framework achieves improved detection rates while minimizing false positives compared to conventional approaches.

ABSTRACT

Phishing attacks have become a major cybersecurity concern in cloud computing environments, where large volumes of confidential data and services are accessed over the internet. As organizations increasingly rely on cloud platforms for storage and communication, attackers exploit this expanded digital space to launch sophisticated phishing campaigns. Conventional security mechanisms that depend on fixed rules or signature-based detection are no longer effective against these evolving threats.

This research presents an adaptive framework designed to predict and detect phishing attacks in cloud environments using advanced data analysis and learning-based techniques. The proposed framework operates through multiple stages, including data cleaning, feature selection, behavioral analysis, and continuous monitoring, to accurately identify malicious activities in emails and URLs. The adaptive nature of the framework allows it to update its detection logic over time, enabling effective recognition of newly emerging phishing patterns and previously unseen attacks.

By deploying the framework within a cloud infrastructure, the system achieves high scalability, rapid threat detection, and efficient resource utilization while maintaining compatibility with existing security systems. Experimental evaluation shows that the proposed approach significantly improves phishing detection accuracy and reduces false alarms when compared to traditional detection methods. Overall, this framework provides a reliable and flexible solution for strengthening security in modern cloud-based systems.

Keywords

Phishing Attacks, Cloud Computing Security, Adaptive Security Framework, Phishing Detection, Phishing Prediction, Machine Learning, Email Security, URL Analysis


Cloud computing has rapidly transformed the way organizations and individuals manage, store, and access data, offering unprecedented scalability, flexibility, and cost efficiency. As businesses migrate critical operations to the cloud, the landscape of cybersecurity threats has evolved in tandem, with phishing attacks emerging as one of the most persistent and damaging risks in these environments. Unlike traditional cyberattacks that exploit technical vulnerabilities, phishing leverages social engineering tactics to manipulate human behavior, making it a formidable challenge for both technical and organizational defenses. The distributed, multi-tenant nature of cloud platforms, combined with the shared responsibility model between providers and users, introduces unique complexities that amplify the impact and detection difficulty of phishing schemes
Despite significant advancements in security technologies, conventional phishing detection methods—such as signature-based filters, blacklists, and static rule-based systems—often fall short in cloud contexts. Attackers now employ sophisticated techniques, including AI-generated messages, deepfakes, and hyper-personalized lures, which can bypass legacy defenses and exploit both technical and human vulnerabilities. As a result, there is a growing consensus among researchers and practitioners that adaptive, context-aware detection frameworks are essential for effective protection in cloud environments. These frameworks must integrate technical solutions with behavioral insights, user education, and continuous learning to address the dynamic nature of phishing threats.
This introduction explores the rise of cloud computing, the severity and evolution of phishing attacks within cloud ecosystems, and the limitations of traditional detection approaches. It underscores the necessity for adaptive, multi-layered defense strategies that account for both technical and social dimensions, setting the stage for a comprehensive review of current research and future directions in phishing mitigation for cloud services.
